I'm a PhD student in Applied Physics at Stanford University, advised by Amir Safavi-Naeini in the [Laboratory for Nanoscale Integrated Quantum Systems](https://linqs.stanford.edu). At Stanford I've focused on making advanced chip-scale coherent light sources that enable applications in portable spectroscopy and sensing. I interned with Apple's Exploratory Design Group (XDG) on integrated biophotonic systems. I'm funded by the NSF GRFP, NTT Research Fellowship, and Stanford Doerr School of Sustainability Accelerator.

Previously, I was a [Churchill Scholar](https://www.churchillscholarship.org/) and MPhil student at the [NanoPhotonics Centre](https://www.np.phy.cam.ac.uk/) (Cambridge), supervised by Jeremy Baumberg. Before that, I studied physics and EE at Rice University, where I worked with [Guru Naik](https://naik.rice.edu/). I spent undergrad research internships working with [Ming Wu](https://www2.eecs.berkeley.edu/Faculty/Homepages/wu.html) (Berkeley, Energy-Efficient Electronics REU), [Kazunari Matsuda](https://kdb.iimc.kyoto-u.ac.jp/profile/en.42a12b7d430d9bc6.html)/[Yuhei Miyauchi](https://kdb.iimc.kyoto-u.ac.jp/profile/en.6598b5bc0640779a.html) (Kyoto, Nakatani RIES REU), and [Nima Aghaeepour](https://nalab.stanford.edu/team/nima-aghaeepour/) (Stanford).

Outside of lab, I love playing chamber music (viola), backpacking, and pickup basketball.
