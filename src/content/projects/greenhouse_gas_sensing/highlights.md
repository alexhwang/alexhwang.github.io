I have led work with Stanford's Doerr [School of Sustainability Accelerator](https://sustainability-accelerator.stanford.edu/project/seeing-light-designing-new-regime-mrv-sensitivity) to utilize emerging integrated nonlinear photonic devices in greenhouse gas sensing applications. Most of our devices have operated in the 2.5-4 um range, where methane, nitrous oxide, water vapor, and carbon dioxide all have strong features.
